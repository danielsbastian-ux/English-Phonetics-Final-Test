MODEL PARAGRAPH — How to Brew a Perfect Cup of Pour-Over Coffee Brewing a well-balanced cup of pour-over coffee requires attention to sequence and timing. First, measure 25 grams of medium-coarse ground coffee and place it in the filter inside the dripper. Next, bring filtered water to approximately 93°C — just below boiling — using a gooseneck kettle. Once the water reaches the right temperature, pour a small amount (about 50 ml) over the grounds in a slow, circular motion to let the coffee bloom for 30 seconds. This step releases carbon dioxide and improves extraction. After the bloom, continue pouring the remaining water in four or five controlled pulses, each time waiting for the water level to drop before adding more. As soon as the last pour drains through, remove the dripper. Finally, gently swirl the cup and allow the coffee to cool for one minute before drinking. Following these steps consistently will produce a clear, aromatic cup every time.
